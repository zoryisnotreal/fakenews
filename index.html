Fake News is “made-up stuff, masterfully manipulated to look like credible
journalistic reports that are easily spread online
to large audiences willing to believe the fictions and spread the word. (Naturale)"

Fake news is a crucial problem in modern society. With the widespread of mobile devices and the increasing reliance on the Internet for reliable information, fake news is the easiest scam to enter society.

Fake news is a complex topic with even more complex consequences.

Fake news can lead to the spread of false information, it can allow a mass group of people to become more susceptible to propaganda, and it can lead to unjust mass reactions (like fear).


In the end, fake news comes in many forms––but the main 4 are:
  1. Misleading websites shared on social media
    Often seen on Facebook, Instagram, etc., these sites rely on “outraged spurred on by false headlines and dubious information.
  2. Websites that circulate misleading/unreliable information
  3. Websites that use sensational headlines and social media descriptions
  4. Satire and comedy sites
    While not intentional, comedy sites can often be used to give commentary on politics and society
    but can also be potentially mistaken for actual news.
